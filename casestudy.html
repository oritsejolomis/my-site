01 Context & Challenge

Nigeria's film industry — Nollywood — is the second-largest in the world by volume, yet its production ecosystem remained fragmented. Filmmakers were losing days sourcing equipment across WhatsApp groups, coordinating crew through disconnected channels, and navigating an absence of verified vendors.

The core challenge: design a platform that could consolidate discovery, booking, and coordination into a single surface without overwhelming users who ranged from independent directors to large studio production teams.

02 Approach

  • 01

    Discovery & Stakeholder Alignment

    Conducted interviews with 14 filmmakers and 8 vendors to map existing workflow pain points, trust signals, and critical booking behaviours across the production lifecycle.

  • 02

    Information Architecture

    Built a taxonomy that separated roles — buyer, seller, crew, studio — into distinct but interoperable contexts, forming the navigation backbone and permission model.

  • 03

    Prototyping & Iteration

    Low-fidelity wireframes focused on task completion speed before moving to a high-fidelity component system built for developer handoff in Figma.

03 The Solution

We delivered a modular dashboard built on a strict 8pt grid system. Each user role receives a contextual view — vendors manage listings and inquiries; filmmakers browse, filter, and book in a single session. A unified design language standardises how listings, profiles, and transaction states are presented across all user types.

04 Outcomes

The platform launched in beta with a curated cohort of Lagos-based vendors and production companies. Early signals pointed to measurable improvements in time-to-booking and vendor conversion from browsing sessions.
